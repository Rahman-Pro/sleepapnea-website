What Is Sleep Apnea?

Understanding the Silent Epidemic

Sleep apnea is a potentially serious sleep disorder in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts throughout the night. Each pause in breathing — called an apnea — can last from a few seconds to minutes, and may occur 30 or more times per hour.

These interruptions prevent you from getting the deep, restorative sleep your body needs, leaving you exhausted during the day and putting your long-term health at serious risk.

  • Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA) — most common type
  • Central Sleep Apnea — brain signaling failure
  • Complex/Mixed Sleep Apnea — combination of both
  • Strongly linked to heart disease, diabetes & stroke
